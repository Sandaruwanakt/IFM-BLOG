Connecting the Dots: What This Means Through an IFM Lens (In-Depth Analysis)

üîÑ Balance of Payments (BOP) Rebalancing in Action

(Image: SUNDAY TIMES)

From an IFM perspective, the balance of payments (BOP) is the central framework to evaluate a country's international economic position. For Sri Lanka, the continuation of monthly current account surpluses in 2025‚Äîdespite a growing merchandise trade deficit‚Äîsignals a significant shift in how the country is adjusting its external sector dynamics.

Key Observations:

  • Trade Deficit widened to USD 472.5 mn in May 2025 (from USD 393.3 mn in May 2024), indicating a net outflow of goods.
  • Yet, the current account recorded a surplus of USD 190.6 mn, showing that services, remittances, and income transfers have more than offset the merchandise gap.

IFM Implication:

This reflects an improvement in current account sustainability, not driven by reduced imports, but through strengthened non-debt inflows. In theory, BOP equilibrium can be maintained via either reducing trade imbalances or increasing "invisible" earnings. Sri Lanka has achieved the latter by enhancing service sector income and diaspora remittances ‚Äî a more sustainable and less contractionary path.

üí± Exchange Rate Dynamics & Foreign Exchange Policy

(IMAGE:SUNDAY TIMES)

The Sri Lankan rupee depreciated by 2.5% YTD against the US dollar, a modest movement suggesting exchange rate flexibility under a managed float regime.

Data Support:

  • End-2024 LKR/USD rate: 292.58
  • End-June 2025 rate: 299.97
  • Depreciation: 7.39 LKR or 2.5%

IFM Implication:

From a policy standpoint, such a contained depreciation indicates that the Central Bank of Sri Lanka (CBSL) is pursuing a "leaning against the wind" approach ‚Äî allowing gradual depreciation to maintain export competitiveness while avoiding inflationary spirals from rising import costs. Maintaining USD 6.3 billion in reserves suggests successful FX intervention to stabilize market sentiment ‚Äî a crucial tool in exchange rate targeting.

This is an excellent case of monetary-exchange rate coordination, helping the country meet dual goals: external competitiveness and price stability.

üåç International Trade: Recovery vs Structural Dependence

(Image: SUNDAY TIMES)

While both exports and imports rose YoY (‚Üë2.3% and ‚Üë7.3%, respectively), the gap between them widened ‚Äî reflecting a deeper trade imbalance and import reliance.

Key Points:

  • Export earnings: USD 1,034.7 mn
  • Import spending: USD 1,507.2 mn
  • Trade deficit: USD 472.5 mn
  • Vehicle imports alone: USD 118 mn

IFM Implication:

The growth in imports, particularly non-essential items like vehicles, reflects domestic demand recovery, but also underlines vulnerability to foreign supply chains and FX outflows. Meanwhile, slower export growth reflects structural limitations in export diversification and value addition ‚Äî common challenges in developing economies.

Further, the terms of trade (TOT) ‚Äî the ratio of export prices to import prices ‚Äî deteriorated, meaning Sri Lanka is paying more for its imports while earning less from exports. This reduces real national income and emphasizes the need for:

  • Export diversification strategies
  • Moving up the global value chain
  • Import substitution where feasible

These are all critical components in international trade strategy under IFM frameworks.

üß≥ Tourism & Remittances: Resilient Buffers Against External Shocks

Tourist arrivals rebounding (Image: Travel voice )

Together, tourism and workers' remittances contributed over USD 805 million in May 2025, becoming the key stabilizers of the current account.

Tourism:

  • Arrivals: 132,919
  • Monthly earnings: USD 164.1 mn
  • Jan‚ÄìMay earnings: USD 1.5 bn

Remittances:

  • May inflow: USD 641.7 mn
  • Jan‚ÄìMay total: USD 3.1 bn (‚Üë18.2% YoY)

IFM Implication:

These inflows are non-debt-creating and countercyclical ‚Äî they increase when domestic economic conditions are weak. In IFM terms, they serve as automatic stabilizers for the BOP, reducing reliance on costly foreign borrowing or reserve drawdowns.

  • Remittances strengthen the secondary income account.
  • Tourism supports the services account ‚Äî a component of invisible trade.

By reducing current account deficits through these avenues, Sri Lanka increases its external sector resilience, much in line with policy prescriptions from IMF stabilization programs.

üìâ Capital Markets: Signs of Confidence with Caveats

Taming behaviour in the face of an untamed index(Image: Unsplash)

May 2025 showed a small but significant net inflow of USD 2.1 mn into capital markets:

  • Govt. Securities: +USD 1.4 mn
  • CSE (equity market): +USD 0.7 mn

However, portfolio investment-related income outflows fell drastically from USD 114.9 mn in May 2024 to just USD 44.0 mn in May 2025 ‚Äî a drop of 61.7%.

IFM Implication:

This drop could be interpreted in two ways:

  1. Short-term capital outflows may have already occurred, and only cautious investors remain.
  2. A global reallocation of capital could be favoring safer markets, indicating a fragile investment climate.

In either case, this highlights the volatile nature of capital account flows, particularly in emerging economies. It reinforces the importance of:

  • Maintaining investor confidence through reforms and transparency
  • Attracting long-term foreign direct investment (FDI) rather than speculative short-term flows

This speaks directly to the IFM concept of capital mobility and financial openness, especially the risks and benefits of integrating into global capital markets.

üì£ Final Word

Sri Lanka's May 2025 performance in the external sector is not just a snapshot ‚Äî it's a lesson in smart economic balancing. While trade imbalances persist, the country has successfully leveraged non-debt creating inflows to maintain stability.

In a world of tightening financial conditions and geopolitical uncertainty, Sri Lanka's external sector story in 2025 proves that with targeted reforms and strategic prioritization, even small economies can punch above their weight.
